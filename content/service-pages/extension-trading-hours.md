## What is Section 59(4)?

### Heading (H2)
**Understanding Extended Trading Hours Applications in Cape Town**

Section 59 of the Liquor Act governs liquor trading hours. Section 59(4) specifically allows licence holders to **apply for permission to trade beyond standard hours** set by provincial legislation.

### Standard Trading Hours (Typical)
These vary by province but generally:
- **Weekdays:** 10:00 - 23:00
- **Fridays:** 10:00 - 00:00 (midnight)
- **Saturdays:** 10:00 - 00:00 (midnight)
- **Sundays & Public Holidays:** Restricted or shorter hours

**Provincial variations:** Each province sets its own standard hours - we know the specifics for your location.

### Extended Trading Hours
With Section 59(4) approval, you can trade:
- Later into the night (01:00, 02:00, or later)
- Earlier in the morning
- On restricted days (Sundays, public holidays)
- Beyond provincial standard limits

**Trading outside approved hours is illegal** and results in fines, licence suspension, or revocation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Heading (H2)
**Extended Trading Hours FAQs**

#### Can I just trade late and apply for permission later?
No. Trading outside your approved hours is illegal, even by one minute. You must have approval before trading extended hours.

#### How late can I trade with extended hours?
This varies by province and location. Some nightclubs receive 02:00 or 04:00 extensions. 24-hour trading is rare but possible. We advise on realistic expectations for your venue.

#### Do extended hours cost more in annual fees?
Yes, licences with extended trading hours typically pay higher annual fees due to increased regulation and monitoring.

#### Will my neighbors object?
Possibly, especially in residential areas. We prepare applications that address neighbor concerns proactively to minimize objections.

#### Can I have extended hours only on weekends?
Yes, you can apply for specific day extensions (e.g., Fridays and Saturdays only).

#### What if I'm denied extended hours?
You can appeal the decision or reapply with improved motivation and mitigation measures. We guide you through this process.

#### Do hotels automatically get extended hours?
Not automatically, but accommodation establishments have stronger grounds for approval due to guest service requirements.
