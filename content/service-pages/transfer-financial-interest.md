## What is Section 51(2)?

### Heading (H2)
**Understanding Financial Interest Transfers in Cape Town**

Section 51(2) governs the **transfer of financial interest in the business** to which a liquor licence relates, without transferring the licence itself (Section 65).

This applies when:
- Adding a new business partner
- Removing an existing partner
- Changing shareholding percentages
- Selling shares in a licensed company
- Restructuring business ownership
- Bringing in new investors
- Buyouts of existing shareholders

**Important:** The licence holder remains the same entity (company/CC), but ownership within that entity changes. This differs from a full licence transfer (Section 65) where the licence moves to a completely different entity.

## Section 51 vs Section 65

### Heading (H2)
**Financial Interest Transfer vs Licence Transfer in Cape Town**

### Section 51(2) - Financial Interest Transfer
- **What changes:** Ownership shares within the licence-holding entity
- **What stays:** The licence-holding entity (company/CC name)
- **When used:** Partnership changes, shareholding adjustments
- **Example:** ABC Restaurant (Pty) Ltd adds a new director/shareholder

### Section 65 - Full Licence Transfer
- **What changes:** The entire licence moves to a different entity/person
- **What stays:** The licensed premises
- **When used:** Selling the entire business to new owners
- **Example:** ABC Restaurant (Pty) Ltd sells to XYZ Hospitality (Pty) Ltd

- No liquor licence violations

New partners/shareholders must meet the same criteria as primary licence holders:

### Citizenship/Residency
- South African citizens
- Permanent residents
- Foreign nationals with valid work permits

- No convictions under Liquor Act
- No serious criminal convictions (case-by-case assessment)
- Clean record preferred

### Financial Standing
- Demonstrate financial stability
- SARS tax compliance
- Not insolvent or under debt review

### Age Requirement
- Must be 18 years or older

### Competence
- Legally competent to enter contracts
- Not under curatorship

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Heading (H2)
**Section 51(2) FAQs**

#### Do all ownership changes require Section 51(2)?
Any change in financial interest (ownership, shareholding, partnership) in a liquor-licensed business requires Section 51(2) approval.

#### How long does Section 51(2) take?
Typically 30-75 days depending on province and documentation completeness.

#### Can I add a silent partner without Section 51(2)?
No. Even non-working partners with financial interest require approval.

#### What if my new partner has a criminal record?
Depends on the nature and timing of convictions. Some records disqualify, others require explanation. We assess eligibility before applying.

#### Do I need Section 51(2) if I'm just changing percentages between existing partners?
Yes, any change in financial interest percentages requires approval.

#### Can Section 51(2) be denied?
Yes, if new parties don't meet requirements or documentation is inadequate. We ensure applications are approval-ready.

#### How much does Section 51(2) cost?
Government fees are R1,000-R2,000 plus professional fees. Far less expensive than full licence transfers.
