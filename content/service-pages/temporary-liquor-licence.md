## What is a Temporary Liquor Licence?

### Heading (H2)
**Understanding Section 48(1) Temporary Licences**

A temporary liquor licence is issued for a **specific limited period**, typically up to 12 months, allowing businesses to sell or serve alcohol legally without applying for a permanent licence.

### When You Need a Temporary Licence

- **Seasonal businesses:** Beach bars, holiday destinations, summer restaurants
- **Pop-up establishments:** Temporary restaurants, bars, or retail
- **Market stalls:** Food and craft markets wanting to serve alcohol
- **Testing viability:** Trial period before committing to permanent licence
- **Short-term leases:** When premises lease is less than 12 months
- **Special trading periods:** Festival season, tourist season, specific months
- **Construction phase:** Trading temporarily while permanent premises is being built

**Note:** This differs from an event liquor licence (Section 48(4)), which is for single events like weddings or festivals.

## Temporary vs Permanent vs Event Licences

### Heading (H2)
**Choosing the Right Licence Type**

### Temporary Liquor Licence (Section 48(1))
- Duration: Up to 12 months
- For ongoing trading during a limited period
- Can be renewed
- Full compliance requirements like permanent licence
- Lower application fees

### Permanent Liquor Licence (Section 36)
- Duration: Indefinite (with annual fees)
- For established long-term businesses
- Higher application fees
- Full permanent investment
- Standard for restaurants, pubs, bottle stores

### Event Liquor Licence (Section 48(4))
- Duration: Single event (1-7 days typically)
- For weddings, festivals, corporate events
- Simplest application process
- Lowest fees
- No permanent premises required

## Application Requirements

### Heading (H2)
**What You Need for a Temporary Liquor Licence**

### Personal Requirements
- South African ID or valid work permit
- No disqualifying criminal record
- Financial standing declaration
- Proof of residence
- SARS tax clearance (may be required)

### Business Requirements
- Business registration (if applicable)
- Temporary trading permit
- Motivation for temporary licence (seasonal nature, limited lease, etc.)
- Business plan or trading proposal
- Proof of insurance

### Premises Requirements
- Lease agreement specifying duration
- Zoning certificate (temporary trading zone or commercial)
- Basic health and safety compliance
- Access and parking considerations
- Distance from schools/churches (provincial variations)

### Application Documentation
- Completed LA1 application form (marked temporary)
- Floor plan or site map
- Specify exact trading dates
- Public notice placement
- Proof of community consultation (some provinces)

Temporary licences carry the same operational compliance requirements as permanent licences:

- Be prepared for inspections

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Heading (H2)
**Temporary Liquor Licence FAQs**

#### Can I get a temporary licence for my food truck?
Yes, if you have designated trading locations and comply with municipal regulations. Mobile traders face additional requirements - we can guide you.

#### How many times can I renew a temporary licence?
This varies by province. Some allow indefinite renewals with proper motivation, others limit you to 2-3 years total temporary trading. We advise based on your province.

#### Is a temporary licence cheaper than a permanent licence?
Yes, significantly. Application fees and annual costs are much lower, making it ideal for seasonal operations.

#### Can I convert a temporary licence to permanent?
Yes, when you're ready for permanent status, you apply for a new permanent licence (Section 36). We handle the transition.

#### What if my temporary period needs to be extended?
You must apply for renewal before your current temporary licence expires. Don't let it lapse - we manage renewals for clients.

#### Do I need a permanent premises for a temporary licence?
No, temporary premises are acceptable as long as they meet basic health, safety, and zoning requirements.
